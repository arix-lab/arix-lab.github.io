Mission

ΛRIX Lab is an independent research initiative exploring AI-native intelligence for physical systems. We develop intelligent systems at the intersection of artificial intelligence, wireless communications, computational imaging, and physical modeling.

Our goal is to create AI methods that understand, predict, and optimize complex physical systems — making connectivity more reliable, intelligent, and accessible for all.

Research Philosophy

How we think about AI and physical systems

Physical systems are governed by fundamental principles — from Maxwell's equations to wave propagation and optical diffraction. At ΛRIX Lab, we combine physics, artificial intelligence, and data-driven learning to create intelligent systems that can perceive, predict, and adapt.


We don't treat AI as a black box. Instead, we build interpretable, physics-aware models that respect the underlying laws of the physical world — enabling trustworthy and deployable intelligent systems.

Research Pillars

Three interconnected areas driving our work

📡 AI-Native Communication Systems

6G networks, V2X communication, joint communication and sensing, digital twins, and near-field electromagnetic systems.

🔬 Computational Imaging & Sensing

Quantitative phase imaging, computational microscopy, biomedical optics, and AI-based image reconstruction.

🧠 Physics-Informed Machine Learning

Machine learning approaches for inverse problems, wave propagation, generative modeling, and scientific discovery.

Featured Projects

Active research initiatives from ΛRIX Lab

Active

Wireless Sensing with CSI

RF/Wireless sensing using Wi-Fi Channel State Information (CSI) and Channel Impulse Response (CIR). Applications include presence detection, gesture recognition, location tracking, vital signs monitoring, and health monitoring systems.

Focus: Presence Detection & Vital Signs Monitoring

Systems Research

Delayed Telemetry & Control-Loop Stability

Investigating how delayed network telemetry affects RL-based load balancing decisions in Open RAN systems. We study xApp/rApp control loops, telemetry delay, and stability conditions for AI-native network automation.

Focus: Open RAN · RL · Load Balancing · Stability Analysis

Flagship Project

WISVA + SCENA

Generative AI (VAE + LSTM) for dynamic wireless connectivity estimation in smart warehouses. Integrated with a Digital Twin and SCENA — an LLM-powered scenario analyzer for natural language querying and prescriptive decision support.

Focus: Digital Twins · GenAI · LLM · Industry 4.0
